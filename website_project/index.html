Mental Health Awareness





Statistics


These are just the statistics of the more common illness:

  • Anxiety: 3.1% of the population have Generalized Anxiety Disorder. If you include all anxiety disorders, including Obsessive Compulsive Disorder, Panic Disorder, and others, that number leaps to 19.1% of the population.
  • Depression: 3.8% of the population have experienced depression. About 20% of those people will be at risk of suicide at some point in their lives.
  • Bipolar Disorder: 0.53% of the population have bipolar disorder. Due to stigmas regarding the condition messing with access to treatment, employment, and education, a person with bipolar will, on average, die 10 years earlier than the general population




    • Misconceptions

    As you can see, mental illness affects many people in a very negative way. However, a lot of the suffering is caused by stigmas the general population has, which are often entirely untrue. For instance:

    • A person with bipolar disorder isn't just someone who get horribly angry and abusive moments. It only means that they have intense mood changes, and the mood itself is different per person
    • People with depression are able to feel other emotions besides sadness. Depression itself is a dampering of every emotion, and is more associated with feelings of hopelessness which lead into sadness than sadness itself. However, those other feelings aren't gone, they're just rarer.
    • Some people think that anxiety is caused by people naturally being more worried, and that there's no real way to treat it. Therapy and medication combined are very useful in treating anxiety for most people, especially in the long term.
    • Generally, there are a lot of people who believe that mental illness isn't real, and that all a person needs is a better diet and healthy living. While those things may help the symptoms, they won't get at the root of the problem.




    Self Treatment

    While the best treatment options are obviously going to therapy and getting treatment from a professional, it's not all hopeless for those who can't afford it. Some things a person can do on their own are:

    • Eating healthy and living healthy are not going to cure you, however for many illnesses, they are a great way to help lessen the symptoms
    • If you are feeling depressed, the best thing to do is talk to someone you know. It doesn't need to be any specific topic, but it's a great way to reassure yourself that you are loved by someone and to distract you from your thoughts.
    • The 333 method of dealing with an anxiety attack: Look around and identify 3 objects, 3 sounds, and then move 3 body parts, in order to ground yourself.
